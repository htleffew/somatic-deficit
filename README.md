# The Somatic Deficit

**Decoupling Moral Ventriloquism from Existential Morality in Large Language Models**

Heather Leffew, PhD. HAIIQU.

This repository holds a theoretical framework and a linked series of preregistered hypotheses about moral representation in large language models. The central claim is that aligned models produce fluent post-conventional moral reasoning while their internal representations stay indifferent to the boundaries that reasoning names. The framework calls this pattern *moral ventriloquism* and argues it is structurally parallel to the semantic dementia Cleckley described in psychopathy, following from training on text that describes moral behavior rather than on the causal and affective architecture that produces it.

## Structure

The program is organized as one theory anchor plus three standalone empirical hypotheses, one per gap the anchor leaves open.

- **Theory anchor.** The Somatic Deficit framework: the Kohlbergian alignment-stage mapping, the moral-ventriloquism claim, and the psychopathy analogue.
- **H1: Ironic Rebound.** Whether semantic suppression instructions paradoxically increase probabilistic anchoring to a forbidden action, and whether structural-skeleton-guided reasoning reduces that rebound.
- **H2: Pre-CoT Commitment.** Whether a moral action choice is already linearly encoded in the residual stream at the pre-generation token, making the subsequent chain of thought a post-hoc rationalization.
- **H3: GRPO Passive-Avoidance Deficit.** Whether GRPO-aligned policies show an algorithmic passive-avoidance deficit under mixed-incentive reward, eroding safety-based punishment boundaries.
